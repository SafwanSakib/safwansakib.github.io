01 — About Me

The Engineer
Behind the Work

A curious mind driven by the challenge of building real, functional systems from first principles.

I'm Safwan Sakib, an undergraduate Mechanical Engineering student at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), currently in my 8th semester with a CGPA of 3.80/4.00.

I thrive at the intersection of robotics, structural design, and simulation. From leading a Mars rover team to international competitions across three continents, to designing a gecko-inspired vertical climbing robot — I turn bold concepts into physical systems that work under pressure.

Beyond engineering, I hold leadership positions across seven student clubs and teams, from President of the Automobile Club to Head of Operations at Team Interplanetar. I believe great engineers are also great communicators, organizers, and collaborators.

My research interests span robotics, additive manufacturing, structural mechanics, convection heat transfer, and solar renewable energy — all grounded in rigorous CAD modeling and FEA simulation.
