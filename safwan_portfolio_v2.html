I'm Safwan Sakib, an undergraduate Mechanical Engineering student at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), currently in my 8th semester. My work sits at the intersection of robotics, autonomous systems, and precision mechanical design.

My thesis focuses on quadcopter UAV systems — developing flight controller algorithms using MPC and LQR techniques, and proposing autonomous navigation with both classical path-planning (A*, RRT*, APF) and Deep Reinforcement Learning (PPO, SAC, TD3). I also work as a Junior Research Fellow at SPARRSO on CubeSat subsystem design and autonomous UAV/UGV platforms.

I lead Team Interplanetar as Head of Operations — directing Mars rovers that have competed in Poland, USA, and Turkey. I hold the presidency of the BUET Automobile Club and the vice-presidency of the BUET Robotics Society. I hold three Certified SolidWorks Professional credentials and have published in IEEE, ICME, and BSME conferences.

Research Experience

Thesis &
Lab Research

Pursuing autonomous flight intelligence — from model-predictive control to deep reinforcement learning on real hardware.

Undergraduate Thesis — Quadcopter UAV Systems
Supervisor: Dr. Kazi Arafat Rahman · Associate Professor, ME Dept, BUET
April 2025 — Present
  • Programmed flight controller algorithms based on MPC and LQR techniques in Python and embedded in custom Pixhawk firmware to enable stable flights and trajectory control
  • Proposed autonomous navigation schemes employing classical (A*, RRT*, APF) and Deep Reinforcement Learning (PPO, SAC, TD3) approaches for flying in known and unknown surroundings
  • Tested algorithms using ROS 2 + Gazebo simulation environment (PX4 SITL) and successfully implemented on a real-life custom quadcopter platform
  • Designed Li-Ion battery model in MATLAB incorporating thermal dynamics through a customized experimental chamber
CFDHT Research Group
Supervisor: Dr. Sumon Saha · Professor, ME Dept, BUET
April 2024 — December 2024
Project: Entropy Generation Analysis in a Ferrofluid filled Stepped Grooved Square Cavity Undergoing MHD and Joule Heating Effects
  • Performed numerical simulation of MHD mixed convection inside stepped grooved cavity using finite element technique (COMSOL Multiphysics)
  • Studied heat transfer and entropy generation for ferrofluid (Fe₃O₄-water) in presence of joule heating and magnetization
  • Results published at 9th BSME International Conference on Thermal Engineering, 2024 — to be published in AIP Proceedings
